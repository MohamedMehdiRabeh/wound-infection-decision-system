# Medical Interpretation

## Purpose
This system simulates how early infection can be detected using physiological signals.

---

## pH Interpretation

- Normal pH indicates stable wound environment
- Rising pH suggests bacterial activity
- High pH may indicate infection progression

Clinical meaning:
- Alkaline shift = possible infection

---

## SpO2 Interpretation

- Measures oxygen saturation in blood
- Low values indicate hypoxia

Clinical meaning:
- Decreasing SpO2 = worsening systemic condition

---

## Symptom Interpretation

- Fever indicates immune response
- Pain reflects tissue inflammation

Combined symptoms increase infection probability

---

## Risk Score Meaning

- Low score → healing or stable condition
- Medium score → early infection risk
- High score → severe infection or systemic involvement

---

## Clinical Insight

This model mimics real-world wound monitoring systems by combining:

- biochemical signals (pH)
- physiological signals (SpO2)
- symptomatic indicators (fever, pain)

to approximate infection progression over time.
